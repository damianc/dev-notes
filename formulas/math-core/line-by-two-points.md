# Linear Function Passing Through 2 Points

$A = (x_1, y_1)$  
$B = (x_2, y_2)$

## Approach #1

$$
a = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}
$$

$$
b = \frac{x_2y_1 - x_1y_2}{x_2-x_1}
$$

$$
\implies
$$

$$
f(x) = ax + b
$$

## Approach #2

$$
m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}
$$

$$
\implies
$$

$$
f(x) = m(x - x_1) + y_1
$$

$$
\cup
$$

$$
f(x) = m(x - x_2) + y_2
$$
